### 9.3 How to Interpret Evidence Quality Ratings

**HIGH Quality:**
- Verifiable data with specific numbers
- Independent source (not vendor-funded)
- Recent (2025-2026) and relevant to current algorithm
- Methodological rigor (for academic sources)
- Cross-referenced by multiple other sources
- **Action:** Treat as strongly evidenced; prioritize these findings in strategy decisions.

**MEDIUM Quality:**
- Credible but limited verification
- May be vendor-funded (flagged with VENDOR CLAIM bias)
- Older data (2022-2024) that may not fully reflect 2026 algorithm
- Single case study or smaller sample size
- **Action:** Cross-reference with HIGH-quality sources before acting. Useful for feature comparisons and tactical ideas.

**LOW Quality:**
- Anecdotal or unverified
- Potentially biased (forum opinions without data)
- Outdated tactics that may no longer work
- Contradicted by higher-quality sources
- **Action:** Treat as preliminary or hypothetical. Do not base major strategy decisions on LOW-quality evidence alone.
